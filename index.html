About Me

I am Nazir S.K., a PhD Research Scholar with an interdisciplinary background in Horticulture, Microbiology, Natural Products Research, and Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR).

My research focuses on the discovery and characterization of bioactive compounds from medicinal and spice plants, essential oils, metabolomics, nanotechnology, and sustainable solutions for combating antimicrobial resistance.

My work integrates microbiology, natural product chemistry, computational biology, metabolomics, and nanotechnology to understand microbial interactions and develop innovative therapeutic approaches.

Beyond research, I enjoy photography, travel, scientific writing, and observing patterns in nature. I believe curiosity, observation, and lifelong learning are the foundations of meaningful scientific discovery.
